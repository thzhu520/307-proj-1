FAQ

What is "The SLOutions"?
"The SLOutions" is a survey website designed to let students, staff, and others on campus anonymously suggest improvements or report problems that need to be addressed. The reports are sent directly to the appropriate authorities to facilitate timely action.
What types of issues can I report?
You can report a wide range of issues, including but not limited to:
  • Maintenance problems (e.g., broken equipment, facilities)
  • Safety concerns (e.g., hazardous areas, poor lighting)
  • Suggestions for policy changes or new initiatives
  • Accessibility issues (e.g., ramps, elevators)
Are there any types of issues I should not report here?
Yes, emergencies requiring immediate attention (such as medical or security incidents) should be reported to 911 or campus security instead of using "The SLOutions."
How are my reports handled?
Once submitted, reports are automatically forwarded to the appropriate department or authority responsible for addressing the issue. The chain of command receives your report directly, ensuring that it reaches the relevant personnel.
Is there a limit to how many reports I can submit?
No, there is no limit. You are welcome to submit as many reports or suggestions as needed.
How long does it take for an issue to be addressed?
Response times vary depending on the nature of the report and the responsible department. Urgent safety concerns are typically prioritized.
Can I provide additional evidence with my report?
Yes, you can include additional details like photos or supporting documents to help authorities address the issue effectively.
Can I edit or withdraw a report after submission?
Currently, reports cannot be edited or withdrawn once submitted. Ensure all details are accurate before submitting.
Can I report an issue outside of campus grounds?
No, "The SLOutions" is intended for campus-related issues only. For off-campus concerns, contact the appropriate local authorities or community organizations.
Can I view reports submitted by others?
No, reports are confidential and cannot be viewed by the public. This ensures privacy for all users.
